The next step was to make a generator that could save images and could handle colors well. I started a new Processing sketch, and created a class for a rainbow palette, and made a Fractal class that generated the mandelbrot set. The Mandelbrot set could be zoomed into similarly to the last generator, but the main improvement was that I could control the speed that the colours are changed (how fast we go through the palette), how many iterations to do, how far offset we want the colours to be. I then added the burning ship fractal. This is a modification of the mandelbrot set, part of what is sometimes known as the "Bad Mandelbrots". It is generated by taking the Modulus of each term at each step, but follows the same general rule. One of the reasons that this fractal is so interesting is that there are new shapes, and many of these are not solid but made up of fairly random colours due to the amount of iterations taken to escape.

It is very common to find repeats of the main shape of the burning ship fractal. Often these look rotated/squished, which is a common feature of the "Bad Mandelbrots". There are also infinitely many of the ships that lie on the real axis, however they get comparitively less wide and taller as you zoom in. This leads to barcode-like patterns.
