Small Electric Car

3D Printer Tiny Car Earbud Case

Sep 2018

I had a few motors and battery packs laying around so I challenged myself to make an electric car with these parts, and to make it as small as I could. I started off by modelling a chassis and some wheels. 3d printed axels wouldn't be strong enough, so I cut 2 lengths off of a metal coat hanger. I then hot glue gunned a wheel to each axel, and put the non driven axel through and glued the other one wheel also. I then removed one half of a 2 x AA battery holder, and resoldered the end that linked to the other. Then, after soldering the switch to the motor, I soldered the switch up to the other end of the battery pack and the battery pack to the motor. Before I fixed the driven axel in place, I needed to print the gear for the motor and glue the motor into a position that would fit with the gear on the wheel. This was the most fiddly part as the wheels were not all that secure on the axels, and any wiggle room on the driven axel could lead to a loss of power. Now I had to fix the battery onto the car. As the chassis was not long enough or wide enough to accomodate both the battery holder and the motor, I hot glued the battery pack to the chassis and the motor at an incline. I then hot glued the switch to the motor.
